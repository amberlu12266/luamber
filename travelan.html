User Research


SURVEYS

I first surveyed 20 participants through social media and forums on Reddit. I screened the participants to narrow them down to people who have planned trip before because I wanted to focus on improving people's experiences with trip planning but not encouraging people to plan trips.

When asked: How often do you plan your trips?
- 32% said they plan 8/10 trips
- 51% said they plan 5/10 trips.
- 18% said they plan 1~2/10 trips.


I dove deeper and asked why not plan all of their trips?
- 63% reported saying that "not all trips require planning"
- 37% said that


Within in the group of people who said they usually plan their trips
- 67% expressed that trip planning is very tiresome and effortful.
- 33% expressed that trip planning isn't tiresome and effortful.



INTERVIEWS

I then conducted follow-up interviews with 5 participants to find out how they currently plan trips and their experiences with the process.


INSIGHT 1: Ways of planning depends on the type of trip

80% of participants revealed that they don't plan ALL of their trips because not all trips require planning.


"I think how much detail I go into when planning a trip really depends on the purpose of the trip. Is it like more casual? Or like we're determined to hit every spot."– Jennifer Tsai


INSIGHT 2: Poor planning is not necessarily a bad thing

Even though 75% of participants responded saying that they've had go on trips that were poorly planned, resulting them to miss out on must-visit destinations, 54% of them reported saying that the spontaneous and casual nature of the trip is what makes it fun.
